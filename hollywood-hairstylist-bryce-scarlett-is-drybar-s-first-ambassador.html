Hollywood Hairstylist Bryce Scarlett Is Drybar's First Ambassador

Publish date: 2024-08-16

If you purchase an independently reviewed product or service through a link on our website, The Hollywood Reporter may receive an affiliate commission.

Drybar was born in 2010 as blowout-only salon for red carpet-ready tresses — and now, the Brentwood-born brand has enlisted its first Hollywood beauty insider to join its squad.

After launching its haircare line in 2013, the brand has tapped celebrity hairstylist Bryce Scarlett — whose clients include Hailey Bieber, Olivia Rodrigo, Margot Robbie, Brie Larson and Kaia Gerber, to name just a few — as an ambassador. (Drybar’s product brand operates as a separate business from the salon chain, which has served stars such as Kristin Chenoweth and Gerber’s model mom, Cindy Crawford.)

Related Stories

The cocktail-inspired label is known for its best-selling haircare products, such as the Detox Dry Shampoo, the Hot Toddy Heat Protectant Mist, the Triple Sec Finishing Spray and its lineup of multi-tasking hair dryer brushes and styling tools. In addition to drybar.com, the vegan and cruelty-free brand is available at online and brick-and-mortar retailers including Amazon, Macy’s, Nordstrom, Sephora, Target and Ulta.

“I’ve always been passionate about the perfect (imperfect) blowout, soft yet full, polished yet lived in,” Scarlett exclusively tells The Hollywood Reporter. “The kind of hair that just gets better the next day. Drybar has been championing the power of a great blowout for more than a decade and I’m so excited to have the opportunity to work alongside them to bring you everything you need for your best blowout.”

His top red carpet-ready trick? “My favorite way to achieve that full, but soft blowout is combining the Detox Dry Shampoo with the Triple Sec 3-in-1 Finishing Spray,” Scarlett explains. The brand doesn’t use ingredients such as phthalates, sulfates, parabens, keratin, nut and mineral oils, petroleum, keratin and other potential irritants or endocrine-disrupting chemicals.

“Drybar truly has the perfect range of products for every hair type and every blowout — and I’m so happy to get to work with them and show everyone. That a great blowout is not one size fits all. I’m excited to help people find what’s right for them and teach them how to achieve it.”

Scarlett was responsible for all of Robbie’s Barbie press tour tresses, from the star’s 1960s-inspired sleek ponytail (a nod to the Mattel toy’s iconic Solo in the Spotlight doll) to her 1984 Day to Night Barbie look. He regularly teams with THR Power Stylists and Beauty Issue pros including makeup artists Nina Park (for clients Brie Larson, Lily-Rose Depp and Olivia Cooke) and Pati Dubroff; stylists such as Samantha McMillan (who also works with Larson), Danielle Goldberg (Gerber, Rodrigo and Lily Aldridge) and Andrew Mukamal (Robbie, Carey Mulligan and others); manicurist Tom Bachik; and others.
