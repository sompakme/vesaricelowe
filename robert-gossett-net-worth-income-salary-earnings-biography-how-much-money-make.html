Robert Gossett (born March 3, 1954) is an American actor who has been on television, in theatre, and in films. After graduating from high school, Gossett got his first professional role in a production of One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest, which he enjoyed immensely. 

He went on to appear in the Broadway productions of Fences by Lloyd Richards, A Raisin in the Sun by Hal Scott, and The Last Minstrel Show by Donald McKayle, among other roles. Also with the Negro Ensemble Company, he appeared in the performances of Manhattan Made Me, The Brothers and the Fathers of Sons, A Soldier’s Play, and Colored People’s Time. Robert also has a wealth of television experience, having appeared in guest parts on shows such as Crossing Jordan, NYPD Blue, and Black Angel. Gossett has appeared in a number of films, including the Jeff Bridges/Tim Robbins picture Arlington Road and the Sandra Bullock thriller The Net.

Gossett is a first cousin of actor Louis Gossett, Jr., who is also a first cousin of Gossett. On TNT’s crime thriller The Closer, as well as its sequel series Major Crimes, he appeared as Commander Russell Taylor. He is married to theatrical director Michele Gossett, and they have two children.
