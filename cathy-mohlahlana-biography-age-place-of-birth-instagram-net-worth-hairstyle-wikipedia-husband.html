Biography

Cathy Mohlahlana (born January 7, 1980s) is a South African award-winning news reporter, journalist, and anchor.

She is well known for being one of the most famous eTV news presenters of all time.

She started her career as a copywriter for online businesses and a radio anchor for her university station.

Popular South African station Radio 702 signed her up immediately after her first year. She is currently working for Newzroom Afrika.

Early Life

Cathy Mohlahlana was born on the 7th of January in the late 1980s in Gauteng, South Africa.

She moved to Polokwane at the young age of six for her boarding school education.

She had a talent for speaking from a young age and was actively involved in her secondary school debating club.

She was also a member of the Model United Nations. She is very private about her age and family and has not released any details concerning her family.

Education

Cathy Mohlahlana completed her Primary School education in Polokwane. She attended Capricon High School in South Africa where she was exposed to public speaking.

She obtained her degree in International Relations and Political Sciences from Wits University.

Career

Cathy Mohlahlana started out as a copywriter at the University. She also worked for her University station and her talents did not go unnoticed. After her first year, Radio 702 gave her full-time employment. She was offered the position of reporter and radio anchor for the station. She briefly worked for Kaya FM at the same time.

Later on, She got a position at eNews Channel Africa (eNCA). She worked there till March 2019. There are rumors that she left the channel due to issues with the station. She resigned along with Thulasizwe Simelane on the 29th day of March 2019.

She is currently working for Newsroom Afrika that airs on DStv channel 405 and hosts Editor’s choice every Sunday morning. She hosts the daily show Newz@Prime that airs between 6:00 pm and 9:00 pm.

Awards

Cathy Mohlahlana won several awards during the course of her career including;

  • National Press Club Radio Journalist of the Year award (2009)
  • Fellow for former US President Barack Obama’s Young Africa Leaders Initiative (2014)
  • CNN Journalism Fellowship (2012)
