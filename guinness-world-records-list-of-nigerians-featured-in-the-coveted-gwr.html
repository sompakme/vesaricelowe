Guinness World Records: List of Nigerians Featured in the Coveted GWR

Publish date: 2024-09-04

Let’s take a moment to marvel at the incredible achievement of Hilda Baci, who has defied all odds and broken the record for the Longest Cooking Marathon by an Individual. With sheer determination and unwavering passion, she surpassed her initial goal of 97 hours and triumphantly reached the awe-inspiring milestone of 100 hours. Today, we spotlight the extraordinary Nigerians who have left an indelible mark on the global stage, setting their sights on greatness and daring to dream.

These remarkable individuals have shattered barriers, surpassed expectations, and etched their names into the prestigious Guinness World Records. Their exceptional accomplishments serve as a beacon of inspiration, igniting the fire within countless others to pursue their own dreams and reach unprecedented heights.

Among these exceptional Nigerians, we find those who fearlessly aimed to surpass existing records, surpassing their own previous achievements and those who proudly remain undefeated in their respective fields. Each one has carved out a unique path, leaving an extraordinary impact on the world around them.

From the legendary music maestros like Fela Kuti and Wizkid, whose melodies resonate through the ages, to the mesmerizing dancers like Kaffy and Pinki Debbie, who effortlessly blend grace and rhythm, to the record-breaking turntable wizards like DJ Obi and DJ Venum, who spin beats that electrify and captivate, this list celebrates the astonishing accomplishments of these Nigerian trailblazers. They have etched their names in the prestigious Guinness World Records, forever solidifying their place in history as pioneers of their craft.

1. Rema

Rema, the sensational music prodigy, propelled himself to unparalleled heights with his smash hit single, Calm Down. This remarkable achievement etched his name in the annals of history as he claimed the coveted title of securing the inaugural No.1 position on the esteemed Official MENA Chart. This groundbreaking regional streaming chart, introduced by the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry on November 29, 2022, showcased the unrivalled talent of Rema, capturing the hearts of audiences across the Middle East and North Africa.

2. Wizkid

Wizkid, the music maestro of Africa, achieved a remarkable milestone by amassing an impressive total of eight All Africa Music Awards (AFRIMA) by 2021. These accolades, bestowed upon him for his exceptional artistry, included three prestigious Artist of the Year titles in 2016, 2017, and 2021.

His musical prowess extended beyond his triumphs as he secured three coveted Song of the Year honours. In 2017, his collaboration with the renowned Drake on the infectious track Come Closer earned him a prestigious accolade. The year 2019 witnessed the undeniable magnetism of his scintillating hit Fever, which garnered well-deserved recognition. And in 2021, Wizkid weaved a melodic masterpiece with the enchanting Essence, featuring the ethereal vocals of Tems, solidifying his position as a musical genius.

3. Tuedon Morgan

Tuedon Morgan embarked on an awe-inspiring journey fueled by a burning desire to reclaim her health and defy the boundaries of human achievement. Little did she know that her personal quest would lead to extraordinary triumphs, etching her name into the annals of athletic history.

With sheer determination, Tuedon Morgan set out to achieve the fastest time to run a half marathon on each continent, as well as conquer the formidable North Pole Marathon. In an astounding display of speed and endurance, she shattered records by completing this incredible feat in a mind-boggling 62 days, 12 hours, 58 minutes, and 49 seconds, spanning from February 8th to April 12th, 2015.

Not only did Tuedon Morgan secure the record for the fastest time to run a half marathon on each continent (female), but she accomplished this remarkable feat in 10 days, 23 hours, 37 minutes, and 8 seconds. Her unwavering determination and spirit propelled her to conquer the challenges before her, leaving a lasting legacy in the realm of athletic greatness.

4. Chinonso Eche

Chinonso Eche, a true maestro of the football world, recently accomplished a remarkable feat that has left fans and onlookers in awe. With his exceptional skills and unwavering focus, he shattered the Guinness World Record for the most consecutive football touches in one minute while expertly balancing the ball on his head.

On a momentous day, February 1, 2023, Chinonso Eche displayed his incredible talent on the grand stage of Lo Show Dei Record in the vibrant city of Milan, Italy. With each touch of the ball, he surpassed his previous record by an impressive 22 touches, leaving spectators mesmerized by his precision and finesse.

Astonishingly, Chinonso Eche achieved a total of 133 consecutive touches, a testament to his exceptional control and mastery of the game. His remarkable achievement has secured his place in the prestigious Guinness World Records and established him as a true icon of football.

12. Adetunwase Adenle

Adetunwase Adenle, a remarkable individual with multiple talents and passions, has etched his name in the prestigious Guinness World Records not once, not twice, but an astounding four times. His unwavering focus on empowering children has led him to accomplish extraordinary feats that testify to his dedication and ingenuity.

The first record-setting achievement occurred during the vibrant “Nigeria at 50 celebrations in 2010. Adetunwase Adenle spearheaded a grand endeavour, rallying 350 Nigerian children who willingly joined forces to create a painting of unparalleled scale. The result was a monumental artwork stretching an impressive 63.5 meters in length and soaring to a height of 49.3 meters. This breathtaking masterpiece not showcased the young participants’ immense artistic talent and symbolized unity, creativity, and the limitless possibilities that can be achieved when dreams intertwine.

Building upon this initial triumph, Adetunwase Adenle ventured into the realm of literacy, recognizing the power of reading to shape young minds. In 2011, he orchestrated a record-breaking gathering of 4,222 children, each immersed in the enchanting world of books. Together, they formed a formidable assembly, united in their passion for literature and collective knowledge pursuit. This extraordinary event set a record and ignited a love for reading among Nigerian students, inspiring them to embark on their own literary journeys.

Undeterred by the scope of his achievements, Adetunwase Adenle continued to innovate, focusing on hygiene and cleanliness. He orchestrated a gathering of children committed to handwashing in a remarkable display of unity and awareness. With a shared purpose and determination, they set a new record for the highest number of children simultaneously practising this vital habit. Adenle‘s efforts promoted cleanliness and fostered a sense of responsibility and well-being among the young participants, shaping their understanding of personal hygiene.

Lastly, during the joyous celebration of Lagos at 50, Adetunwase Adenle unleashed his boundless creativity again. With an artistic vision that knew no limits, he crafted the largest post office stamp ever conceived, capturing the essence and spirit of Lagos. This larger-than-life creation symbolized the city’s rich heritage and served as a testament to Adenle‘s artistic prowess and ability to leave an indelible mark on the canvas of history.
