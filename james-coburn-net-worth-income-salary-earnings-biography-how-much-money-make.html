James Harrison Coburn III (August 31, 1928 – November 18, 2002) was an American actor who appeared in films and television shows throughout his career. He starred in roughly 70 films and more than 100 television appearances throughout his 45-year career, earning an Academy Award for his supporting performance as Glen Whitehouse in Affliction. Coburn was born in New York City and raised in California. He was a capable, rough-hewn leading man whose toothy grin and lanky body made him a perfect tough-guy in a variety of leading and supporting roles in westerns and action films, including The Magnificent Seven, Snow Dogs, Hell Is for Heroes, The Great Escape, Major Dundee, Our Man Flint, In Like Flint, Duck, You Sucker!, Pat Garrett and Billy the Kid, Charade, and Cross of Iron.  He was also a leading actor in As a result of his work with Lee Marvin, Steve McQueen, and Charles Bronson in the late 1960s and early 1970s, he established himself as one of the leading “tough-guy” performers of his generation, alongside Lee Marvin, Steve McQueen, and Charles Bronson, among others.
