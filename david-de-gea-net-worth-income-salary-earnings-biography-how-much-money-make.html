David de Gea Quintana (Spanish pronunciation: [dai e xea kintana]; born November 7, 1990) is a Spanish professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for the English club Manchester United. De Gea Quintana was born in Madrid, Spain, on November 7, 1990.Born in Madrid, he began his professional career with Atlético Madrid when he was ten years old, progressing through the club’s youth system until making his senior debut in 200After being promoted as Atlético’s first-choice goalkeeper, he assisted the club in winning both the UEFA Europa League and the UEFA Super Cup in the same season. As a result of his performances, he caught the attention of Manchester United, who signed him in June 2011 for £17.8 million, breaking the British record for a goalie at the time. Spanish under-21 captain David de Gea captained his country’s under-21 national team to two European Championship titles in 2011 and 2013, as well as a participation in the 2012 Olympics. In 2014, he made his senior squad debut and was picked to represent his country at the 2014 World Cup in Brazil.
