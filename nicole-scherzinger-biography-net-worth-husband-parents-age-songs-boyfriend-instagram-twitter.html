Nicole Scherzinger is an American artist known for singing, songwriting, dancing, acting, and television-hosting roles.

She was part of the popular girl group The Pussycat Dolls, which has sold over 55 million records globally, making them one of the most successful female bands in history.

Early Life

Nicole Prascovia Elikolani Valiente was born in Honolulu, Hawaii, on June 29, 1978. Her father, Alfonso Valiente, is of Filipino descent, and her mother, Rosemary Elikolani Frederick, has Native Hawaiian and Ukrainian roots.

After her parents’ separation, Rosemary married Gary Scherzinger, a German-American who adopted Nicole, leading her to take on the Scherzinger surname. She has a younger sister named Keala.

The family moved to Louisville, Kentucky, when Nicole was six, where her mother worked as a clerk and her stepfather as a welder.

Despite financial challenges, Nicole helped support her family by waitressing, doing local modeling gigs, and performing at Kentucky Kingdom theme park.

Raised Catholic due to her grandfather’s influence, she considered herself conservative with deeply held religious beliefs and attended church frequently to honor her mother.

Education

She trained at the Youth Performing Arts School at duPont Manual High School in Louisville, Kentucky, and was involved with the Actors Theatre of Louisville.

She received a scholarship to Wright State University in Dayton, Ohio, where she studied Theatre Arts with a minor in Dance.

In college, she starred in leading roles in various musical productions.

Despite leaving college before graduating to pursue a music career with Days of the New, she has stayed connected to Wright State University. 2013, she founded the “Nicole Scherzinger YPAS Scholarship Fund” to aid upcoming arts students.

Career

Scherzinger’s career started as a backing vocalist for Days of the New in the late 1990s, followed by a stint in the reality TV group Eden’s Crush.

However, her big break came when she became the lead singer of The Pussycat Dolls, helping them become one of the best-selling girl groups ever with hits like “Don’t Cha,” “Buttons,” “When I Grow Up,” and “Jai Ho! (You Are My Destiny).”

Scherzinger also has a successful solo career, with albums like “Killer Love” and “Big Fat Lie,” and hits such as “Baby Love,” “Right There,” and “Your Love.”

Scherzinger was crowned the winner of the tenth season of Dancing with the Stars in 2010. Additionally, she has been a judge on various television talent shows such as The Sing-Off from 2009 to 2010, The X Factor US in 2011,

The X Factor UK from 2012 to 2013, 2016 to 2017, and in 2019, as well as Australia’s Got Talent in 2019.

Scherzinger debuted in the West End when she starred in the revival of Cats in 2014, earning a nomination for a Laurence Olivier Award for her role as Best Actress in a Supporting Role in a Musical.

Personal Life

Nicole Scherzinger has been a public figure for years, and while she does share some details of her personal life, she also values her privacy.

In 2020, she began a relationship with former Scottish rugby player Thom Evans, and they got engaged in June 2023.

Before this, she had a long-term, on-and-off relationship with Formula 1 driver Lewis Hamilton, which ended in 2015.

She also dated Bulgarian tennis player Grigor Dimitrov from 2016 to 2019 and was engaged to American musician Nick Hexum from 2000 to 2004.
