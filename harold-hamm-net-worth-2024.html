Who is Harold Hamm?

Harold Hamm is best known as the founder and CEO of Continental Resources, an oil and natural gas company that has been a key player in the development of the Bakken formation in North Dakota and Montana. His journey from humble beginnings to becoming one of the wealthiest individuals in the United States is a classic tale of American entrepreneurship.

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Hamm’s story began in Lexington, Oklahoma, where he was born as the youngest of thirteen children to sharecroppers. Despite not having a college degree, Hamm’s tenacity and business acumen led him to start his own trucking company, which eventually evolved into Continental Resources in 1967. His early recognition of the potential in horizontal drilling and hydraulic fracturing positioned him to capitalize on the shale boom that would transform the American energy landscape.

The Rise of Continental Resources

Under Hamm’s leadership, Continental Resources grew from a small-time operation into a leading upstream oil and natural gas company. The company’s success is largely attributed to its significant presence in the Bakken formation, where it is one of the largest leaseholders and producers of oil.
