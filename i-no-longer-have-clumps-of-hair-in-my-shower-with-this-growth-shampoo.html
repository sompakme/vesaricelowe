I No Longer Have Clumps of Hair in My Shower With This Growth Shampoo

Publish date: 2024-07-31

The way my hair has started shedding as I get older is horrifying. I think it’s a combination of having had COVID-19 and the hormonal issues associated with polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) that causes hair loss. The scariest moment of my day is getting ready to step out of the shower and seeing a giant clump of hair covering the drain. But the clump has been shrunk into just a few strands of hair and my routine hasn’t changed — except for the addition of dpHue’s ACV Revitalizing Shampoo.

DpHue’s Revitalizing Shampoo is part of its apple cider vinegar line (hence, ACV). It has a water-like texture that foams, but in my experience, only when you’re really putting in elbow grease and doing a good job of scrubbing your scalp and massaging your strands. I noticed a difference in texture after my first wash, but I really started to see improvements in hair shedding and the overall plumpness of my strands after five washes. I realized after using this that my hair was feeling weighed down, the ACV wash made it so weightless, which improved my curls. 

The important ingredients at play here are the titular apple cider vinegar, hyaluronic acid, and peptides. First of all, ACV helps with dandruff. It’s not sexy to admit this but that is one of the banes of my existence and it has definitely improved with this shampoo. Studies have also shown that it really removes debris, gunk, and buildup as well as makes strands healthier (aka silky, glistening, and soft). Hyaluronic acid works on your scalp similarly to how it works on your face, it draws in moisture and helps tame frizz. One study found that peptides help with hair loss by increasing the actual number of hairs. 

That’s a lot of information but in summation, science says that these ingredients should help with hair health and even growth. Head to Dermstore or dpHue’s website to shop its ACV Revitalizing Shampoo. (P.S. There’s a conditioner, too.)
